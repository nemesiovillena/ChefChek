# Conservation Zone Mapping System

## Overview

The conservation zone mapping system classifies all ingredients, products, and materials by their optimal storage conditions. This classification enables efficient inventory management, proper receiving procedures, and optimal order consolidation for delivery logistics.

## Zone Definitions

### Zone 1: FROZEN (-18°C or lower)

**Storage Requirements**:
- Temperature: -18°C to -22°C (0°F to -8°F)
- Humidity: 85-95%
- Air circulation: Good circulation recommended
- Shelf life: 6-12 months (varies by product)

**Product Categories**:
| Subcategory | Examples                            | Typical Shelf Life | Handling Notes                          |
|-------------|-------------------------------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Meats       | Beef, pork, poultry cuts            | 6-9 months        | Vacuum-seal for longer life            |
| Seafood     | Fish fillets, shrimp, shellfish     | 3-6 months        | Use within 3-6 months for best quality  |
| Vegetables  | Peas, corn, mixed vegetables        | 8-12 months       | Blanch before freezing                  |
| Fruits      | Berries, sliced fruits              | 6-12 months       | Add ascorbic acid to prevent browning   |
| Prepared    | Prepared meals, sauces              | 3-6 months        | Cool before freezing                    |
| Desserts    | Ice cream, frozen desserts          | 6-12 months       | Maintain constant temperature          |
| Dough       | Pizza dough, pastry dough           | 3-6 months        | Proof before use                        |

**Receiving Guidelines**:
1. Check temperature upon delivery: must be ≤ -12°C
2. Verify packaging integrity (no tears, frost)
3. Check for ice crystals (indicates thawing/refreezing)
4. Record temperature and time of receipt
5. Transfer to freezer within 30 minutes

**Quality Indicators**:
- **Acceptable**: Clean, no freezer burn, solid texture
- **Reject**: Thawed, ice crystals, freezer burn, off-odor

### Zone 2: REFRIGERATED (4°C)

**Storage Requirements**:
- Temperature: 1°C to 4°C (34°F to 40°F)
- Humidity: 85-95%
- Air circulation: Moderate circulation
- Shelf life: 1-7 days (highly variable)

**Product Categories**:
| Subcategory | Examples                            | Typical Shelf Life | Handling Notes                          |
|-------------|-------------------------------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Fresh Meats | Beef, pork, lamb cuts               | 3-5 days          | Store on bottom shelf                   |
| Poultry     | Chicken, turkey, duck                | 2-4 days          | Use within 2-3 days for best quality    |
| Fresh Seafood| Fish, shellfish, crustaceans       | 1-2 days          | Use immediately, very perishable         |
| Dairy       | Milk, cream, yogurt                 | 5-7 days          | Check expiration dates                  |
| Cheese      | Soft cheese, fresh cheese           | 3-7 days          | Wrap in wax paper                       |
| Eggs        | Fresh eggs                           | 3-5 weeks         | Store in original carton                |
| Produce     | Leafy greens, berries, herbs        | 3-5 days          | Keep dry, use humidity drawers           |
| Prepared    | Cooked foods, leftovers             | 3-4 days          | Label with preparation date             |

**Receiving Guidelines**:
1. Check temperature: must be ≤ 5°C
2. Verify product is chilled to touch
3. Check expiration dates (if applicable)
4. Inspect for damage or spoilage
5. Transfer to refrigerator within 30 minutes

**Quality Indicators**:
- **Acceptable**: Cool to touch, no off-odors, no visible spoilage
- **Reject**: Warm, off-odors, mold, discoloration, slime

### Zone 3: DRY_GOODS (15-20°C)

**Storage Requirements**:
- Temperature: 15°C to 20°C (59°F to 68°F)
- Humidity: 50-60%
- Air circulation: Good circulation
- Shelf life: 6-24 months (varies by product)

**Product Categories**:
| Subcategory | Examples                            | Typical Shelf Life | Handling Notes                          |
|-------------|-------------------------------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Grains      | Rice, pasta, flour, oats           | 12-24 months      | Store in airtight containers            |
| Legumes     | Beans, lentils, peas                | 12-18 months      | Store in airtight containers            |
| Spices      | Herbs, spices, seasoning blends     | 6-12 months       | Store in dark, cool place               |
| Oils        | Olive oil, vegetable oil            | 12-18 months      | Protect from light                      |
| Nuts/Seeds  | Almonds, walnuts, seeds             | 6-12 months       | Store in airtight containers            |
| Canned      | Canned vegetables, fruits, sauces   | 12-24 months      | Check for dents, swelling               |
| Dried       | Dried fruits, jerky                 | 6-12 months       | Check for moisture                      |
| Baking      | Sugar, salt, leavening agents       | 12-24 months      | Store in airtight containers            |

**Receiving Guidelines**:
1. Check packaging integrity (no holes, tears)
2. Verify expiration dates (if applicable)
3. Inspect for pests or pest damage
4. Check for moisture damage or mold
5. Store in cool, dry, dark place

**Quality Indicators**:
- **Acceptable**: Clean packaging, no pests, no mold
- **Reject**: Damaged packaging, pests, mold, rancid odor

### Zone 4: AMBIENT (20-25°C)

**Storage Requirements**:
- Temperature: 20°C to 25°C (68°F to 77°F)
- Humidity: 40-50%
- Air circulation: Good circulation
- Shelf life: 6-36 months

**Product Categories**:
| Subcategory | Examples                            | Typical Shelf Life | Handling Notes                          |
|-------------|-------------------------------------|-------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Non-Perishable| Shelf-stable foods, crackers     | 6-12 months       | Keep in original packaging              |
| Snacks      | Chips, cookies, candy              | 6-12 months       | Keep sealed to maintain freshness        |
| Beverages   | Bottled water, soda, juice         | 12-24 months      | Check for leaks, swelling               |
| Condiments  | Ketchup, mustard, sauces            | 12-24 months      | Check for seal integrity                 |
| Household   | Cleaning supplies, paper products   | 24-36 months      | Keep separate from food areas            |
| Packaging   | Containers, disposables, labels     | 24-36 months      | Keep dry and organized                   |

**Receiving Guidelines**:
1. Check packaging integrity
2. Verify expiration dates (if applicable)
3. Check for damage or crushing
4. Verify proper labeling
5. Store in organized, accessible manner

**Quality Indicators**:
- **Acceptable**: Clean packaging, intact seals, no damage
- **Reject**: Damaged packaging, missing seals, leaks

### Zone 5: SPECIAL (Variable)

**Storage Requirements**:
- Temperature: Variable (see product-specific requirements)
- Humidity: Variable
- Air circulation: Product-specific
- Shelf life: Variable

**Product Categories**:
| Subcategory | Examples                            | Storage Requirements | Handling Notes                          |
|-------------|-------------------------------------|----------------------|-----------------------------------------|
| Hazardous   | Cleaning chemicals, solvents       | Ventilated area      | Keep away from food areas              |
| Alcohol     | Spirits, wine, beer                | 10-15°C, dark        | Store away from light                   |
| Delicate    | Specialty cheeses, truffles        | 8-12°C, high humidity| Follow supplier guidelines              |
| Live        | Shellfish, oysters                 | Live holding tank    | Keep in seawater, monitor temperature   |
| Lab Samples | Test products, samples             | Controlled access    | Follow safety protocols                 |

**Receiving Guidelines**:
1. Follow supplier-specific instructions
2. Verify proper documentation/permits (if required)
3. Check for special handling requirements
4. Store in designated specialized area
5. Monitor conditions regularly

**Quality Indicators**:
- **Acceptable**: Meets supplier specifications
- **Reject**: Does not meet specifications, damaged

## Zone Priority System

### Order Consolidation Priority

When consolidating orders for delivery, zones are prioritized:

```
Priority 1: FROZEN (critical temperature control)
  └── Requires refrigerated trucks with separate freezer compartment
  └── Must be delivered first to maintain cold chain
  └── Unacceptable to combine with non-frozen items

Priority 2: REFRIGERATED (perishable, short shelf life)
  └── Requires refrigerated truck
  └── Must be delivered within 4 hours of pickup
  └── Can be combined with other refrigerated items

Priority 3: DRY_GOODS (quality-sensitive but shelf-stable)
  └── Standard delivery acceptable
  └── Can be combined with ambient items
  └── Protect from moisture and extreme temperatures

Priority 4: AMBIENT (non-perishable)
  └── Standard delivery acceptable
  └── Flexible delivery windows
  └── Lowest priority for urgent delivery

Priority 5: SPECIAL (product-specific)
  └── Follow product-specific requirements
  └── May require special handling/certifications
  └── Cannot be combined with other items
```

## Product to Zone Mapping Algorithm

### Automatic Classification

```typescript
function determineConservationZone(product: Product): ConservationZone {
  // Rule 1: Explicit zone assignment (highest priority)
  if (product.conservationZone) {
    return product.conservationZone
  }

  // Rule 2: Category-based classification
  const zoneByCategory = {
    FROZEN: ['frozen_meat', 'frozen_seafood', 'frozen_vegetables', 'ice_cream'],
    REFRIGERATED: ['fresh_meat', 'fresh_seafood', 'dairy', 'eggs', 'fresh_produce'],
    DRY_GOODS: ['grains', 'legumes', 'spices', 'canned_goods', 'baking_ingredients'],
    AMBIENT: ['snacks', 'beverages', 'condiments', 'packaging_materials'],
    SPECIAL: ['hazardous', 'alcohol', 'live_products']
  }

  for (const [zone, categories] of Object.entries(zoneByCategory)) {
    if (categories.includes(product.category)) {
      return zone as ConservationZone
    }
  }

  // Rule 3: Supplier recommendation
  if (product.supplierRecommendedZone) {
    return product.supplierRecommendedZone
  }

  // Rule 4: Default to AMBIENT
  return ConservationZone.AMBIENT
}
```

## Temperature Monitoring

### Monitoring Points

Each zone requires temperature monitoring:

| Zone        | Monitoring Frequency | Alert Threshold     | Critical Threshold   |
|-------------|----------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| FROZEN      | Every 15 minutes     | > -15°C             | > -12°C              |
| REFRIGERATED| Every 30 minutes     | > 5°C               | > 7°C                |
| DRY_GOODS   | Every 2 hours        | < 10°C OR > 25°C    | < 5°C OR > 30°C      |
| AMBIENT     | Every 4 hours        | < 15°C OR > 30°C    | < 10°C OR > 35°C     |
| SPECIAL     | Product-specific     | Product-specific    | Product-specific     |

2. **15 min unacknowledged**: Escalate to head chef
3. **30 min unacknowledged**: Escalate to management
4. **60 min unacknowledged**: Escalate to owner/GM

## Cross-Contamination Prevention

### Zone Separation

**Physical Barriers**:
- Separate storage rooms for each zone
- Airlocks between zones
- Separate equipment per zone
- Color-coded storage containers

**Workflow Rules**:
- Staff wash hands when moving between zones
- Equipment sanitized before use in different zones
- FIFO (First In, First Out) applied separately per zone
- No mixing of zones during storage

### Allergen Control

**Allergen-Designated Areas**:
- DRY_GOODS zone can have allergen-designated shelves
- Separate utensils and containers
- Clear labeling of allergen-containing products
- Sanitization protocols for allergen areas

```sql
CREATE TYPE ConservationZone AS ENUM (
  'FROZEN',
  'REFRIGERATED',
  'DRY_GOODS',
  'AMBIENT',
  'SPECIAL'
);

CREATE TABLE ConservationZoneConfig (
  id SERIAL PRIMARY KEY,
  zone ConservationZone NOT NULL UNIQUE,
  
  -- Temperature Requirements
  minTemperature DECIMAL(5,2),
  maxTemperature DECIMAL(5,2),
  targetTemperature DECIMAL(5,2),
  
  -- Humidity Requirements
  minHumidity DECIMAL(5,2),
  maxHumidity DECIMAL(5,2),
  targetHumidity DECIMAL(5,2),
  
  -- Air Circulation
  airCirculation VARCHAR(50),
  
  -- Delivery Requirements
  vehicleType VARCHAR(100),
  deliveryWindowHours INT,
  requiresRefrigeration BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  
  -- Monitoring
  monitoringFrequencyMinutes INT,
  warningThresholdTemperature DECIMAL(5,2),
  criticalThresholdTemperature DECIMAL(5,2),
  
  -- Inventory Management
  reorderPointPercentage DECIMAL(5,2),
  emergencyOrderPercentage DECIMAL(5,2),
  warningDaysBeforeExpiry INT,
  criticalDaysBeforeExpiry INT,
  
  -- Metadata
  description TEXT,
  active BOOLEAN DEFAULT true,
  createdAt TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
  updatedAt TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP
);

CREATE TABLE ProductZoneMapping (
  id VARCHAR(36) PRIMARY KEY,
  productId VARCHAR(36) NOT NULL,
  zone ConservationZone NOT NULL,
  
  -- Override Information
  isOverridden BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  overrideReason TEXT,
  overriddenBy VARCHAR(100),
  overriddenAt TIMESTAMP,
  
  -- Validation
  isValidated BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  validatedBy VARCHAR(100),
  validatedAt TIMESTAMP,
  
  -- Metadata
  notes TEXT,
  createdAt TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
  updatedAt TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
  
  FOREIGN KEY (productId) REFERENCES Product(id),
  FOREIGN KEY (zone) REFERENCES ConservationZoneConfig(zone),
  UNIQUE (productId),
  INDEX idx_product_id (productId),
  INDEX idx_zone (zone)
);

CREATE TABLE TemperatureLog (
  id VARCHAR(36) PRIMARY KEY,
  zone ConservationZone NOT NULL,
  temperature DECIMAL(5,2),
  humidity DECIMAL(5,2),
  
  -- Timestamp
  recordedAt TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
  
  -- Sensor Information
  sensorId VARCHAR(100),
  sensorLocation VARCHAR(255),
  
  -- Alert Status
  isAlert BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  alertType VARCHAR(20),
  acknowledged BOOLEAN DEFAULT false,
  acknowledgedBy VARCHAR(100),
  acknowledgedAt TIMESTAMP,
  resolvedAt TIMESTAMP,
  
  -- Notes
  notes TEXT,
  
  FOREIGN KEY (zone) REFERENCES ConservationZoneConfig(zone),
  INDEX idx_zone_recorded (zone, recordedAt),
  INDEX idx_alert (isAlert, acknowledged)
);

CREATE TABLE ZoneDeliverySchedule (
  id VARCHAR(36) PRIMARY KEY,
  orderId VARCHAR(36) NOT NULL,
  zone ConservationZone NOT NULL,
  
  -- Scheduling
  scheduledTime TIMESTAMP NOT NULL,
  estimatedArrivalTime TIMESTAMP NOT NULL,
  actualArrivalTime TIMESTAMP,
  
  -- Status
  status VARCHAR(20) DEFAULT 'SCHEDULED',
  
  -- Vehicle Information
  vehicleType VARCHAR(100),
  vehicleNumber VARCHAR(50),
  driverName VARCHAR(255),
  driverPhone VARCHAR(20),
  
  -- Quality Control
  temperatureOnArrival DECIMAL(5,2),
  temperatureOnDeparture DECIMAL(5,2),
  qualityCheckPassed BOOLEAN,
  qualityCheckNotes TEXT,
  
  -- Issues
  issuesEncountered TEXT,
  resolution TEXT,
  
  -- Metadata
  createdAt TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
  updatedAt TIMESTAMP DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
  
  FOREIGN KEY (orderId) REFERENCES AutomatedOrder(id),
  FOREIGN KEY (zone) REFERENCES ConservationZoneConfig(zone),
  INDEX idx_order_id (orderId),
  INDEX idx_zone_status (zone, status),
  INDEX idx_scheduled (scheduledTime)
);
```

## Best Practices

### For Kitchen Staff

1. **Know Your Zones**: Memorize which zone each product belongs to
2. **Respect Boundaries**: Never store products in wrong zones
3. **Report Issues**: Immediately report temperature or quality issues
4. **Follow FIFO**: Always use first-in-first-out per zone
5. **Label Everything**: Mark all containers with zone and date

### For Receiving Staff

1. **Check Temperature**: Verify delivery temperature meets zone requirements
2. **Zone Immediately**: Transfer products to correct zone within 30 minutes
3. **Inspect Quality**: Check for signs of temperature abuse or spoilage
4. **Document Everything**: Record temperatures, times, and any issues
5. **Communicate**: Alert kitchen of any delivery issues

### For Inventory Managers

1. **Monitor Trends**: Track temperature and quality issues per zone
2. **Maintain Equipment**: Ensure refrigeration equipment is functioning properly
3. **Plan Deliveries**: Schedule zone-appropriate delivery times
4. **Train Staff**: Regular training on zone procedures
5. **Audit Regularly**: Weekly audits of zone compliance

## Troubleshooting

### Common Issues

**Issue: Product in wrong zone**
- Solution: Immediately move to correct zone
- Solution: Check for quality damage
- Solution: Report to manager if quality compromised

**Issue: Temperature alert triggered**
- Solution: Check all products in zone
- Solution: Transfer unaffected items to backup storage
- Solution: Call for equipment maintenance

**Issue: Zone confusion during receiving**
- Solution: Refer to product-zone mapping chart
- Solution: Check product labels and packaging
- Solution: Contact supplier if unclear

**Issue: Cross-contamination risk**
- Solution: Separate items by zone immediately
- Solution: Clean and sanitize storage area
- Solution: Discard compromised items
